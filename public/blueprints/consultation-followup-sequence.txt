THE POST-CONSULTATION FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE
A three-email template set for solo attorneys
Built and tested by James at The Automated Solo (theautomatedsolo.com)

---
HOW TO USE THIS FILE
---

Load these three emails into a SendFox automation triggered by being added
to a list called "Post-Consultation." Swap out anything in [brackets] for
your actual details. The send timing is noted above each email.

One thing worth knowing before you start: plain-text emails consistently
outperform HTML templates for personal follow-up. Do not add a header logo.
Do not add a footer with unsubscribe links at the bottom of Email 1. These
are not marketing emails. They are personal notes from an attorney.

---
EMAIL 1: Day-Of Recap
Send timing: Fires immediately when contact is added to the list
Subject: Great talking today
---

Hi [First Name],

Really enjoyed our conversation today. I appreciate you taking the time.

If you want to move forward, here is the next step: [link to retainer
agreement or intake form]. The process is straightforward and I will walk
you through anything you have questions about.

Feel free to reply here if you want to talk through anything first.

[Your Name]
[Your Phone Number]

---
EMAIL 2: 72-Hour Check-In
Send timing: 72 hours after Email 1
Subject: Re: Great talking today
---

Hi [First Name],

Just checking in from our conversation earlier this week.

If you are still thinking things through, no pressure at all. If a question
came up that I did not fully cover, just hit reply and I will get back to you
same day.

The retainer link is below whenever you are ready: [retainer or intake link]

[Your Name]

---
EMAIL 3: Day-Seven Close
Send timing: 7 days after Email 1
Subject: Closing the loop
---

Hi [First Name],

I am wrapping up new client intake for this month and wanted to reach out
one last time.

If the timing is not right, completely understood. If you do want to move
forward, today is a good day to do it: [retainer or intake link]

Either way, I hope our conversation was helpful.

[Your Name]

---
A FEW NOTES ON HOW I ACTUALLY USE THESE
---

The subject lines are intentionally plain. No "Following up on our
consultation" with the date formatted like a CRM entry. Clients receive
enough formal email. These land differently when they look like a personal
note from someone who actually cares.

The three-email structure is deliberate. More than three emails and you
start to feel like a sales funnel. Fewer than three and you leave conversions
on the table. I have tested both directions on this and the three-email
sequence converts better than anything else I have tried.

One more thing: if a prospect replies to any of these emails, have a Typedesk
snippet ready to go. The /yes-retainer snippet for "I want to move forward,"
the /more-questions snippet for anyone who needs more information, and the
/not-yet snippet for gracious declines that move them to a long-term nurture
list. You should not be typing those replies from scratch every time.
