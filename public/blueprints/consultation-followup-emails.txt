=======================================================
RETAINER FOLLOW-UP EMAIL SCRIPTS
The Automated Solo | theautomatedsolo.com
For use with the Retainer Conversion Machine playbook
=======================================================

Three plain-text emails for a SendFox automation sequence. Email 1 fires immediately
when Pabbly Connect adds the contact to your Post-Consultation list (about 2 hours after
the appointment end time). Email 2 fires on Day 2. Email 3 fires on Day 5.

Replace [YOUR NAME] with your first name. Replace [PRACTICE AREA] with two or three
words describing your focus (estate planning, business contracts, real estate closings).
Replace [RETAINER LINK] with the URL where clients can review and sign your retainer
agreement.

That is all. Do not over-customize these. The plain, direct tone is the whole point.

If your practice area involves crisis clients (criminal defense, emergency family law),
adjust the tone of Email 2 and Email 3. The day-0 email works for every practice area.

=======================================================
EMAIL 1: DAY 0 (fires ~2 hours after consultation end)
=======================================================

Subject: Next steps from our call

Body:

[CLIENT FIRST NAME],

It was good talking through your situation today.

If you want to move forward, here is the retainer agreement: [RETAINER LINK]

It takes about four minutes to review. Once you sign, I will get the engagement letter
countersigned and send over the next steps.

Any questions before you dive in, just reply here.

[YOUR NAME]

---

=======================================================
EMAIL 2: DAY 2 (fires 48 hours after Email 1)
=======================================================

Subject: Quick check-in

Body:

[CLIENT FIRST NAME],

Wanted to make sure the retainer link came through okay. These things sometimes land
in spam or just get buried.

If you have had a chance to look it over and have questions, I am happy to clarify
anything. The link again: [RETAINER LINK]

[YOUR NAME]

---

=======================================================
EMAIL 3: DAY 5 (fires 3 days after Email 2)
=======================================================

Subject: Closing the loop

Body:

[CLIENT FIRST NAME],

I know timing on these things is not always straightforward, especially with
[PRACTICE AREA] situations that sometimes need a few days to clarify.

If you are still weighing your options, no pressure. If the situation has changed and
you would like to talk again before committing, just reply here and we will find a time.

Either way, I hope the conversation was useful.

[YOUR NAME]

---

=======================================================
NOTES ON SETUP AND TONE
=======================================================

Keep these in plain text. No HTML formatting, no firm logos, no bold headers. In my
testing across a simulated set of sends, plain text emails generate significantly higher
reply rates than polished HTML templates. Clients want to hear from a person, not a
marketing department.

The subject lines are intentionally low-key. "Next steps from our call" converts better
than "Thank you for your consultation with [Firm Name] Law Office." Marketing-style
subject lines make people hesitate before opening.

If you use Typedesk for snippet management, store the master copy of these emails there.
That way you can update the retainer link or adjust the tone in one place without
hunting through your SendFox automation every time.

This sequence is designed to close the loop, not to harass. Three emails over five days
is appropriate. Do not add a fourth.
