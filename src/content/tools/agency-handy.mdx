## The Setup: Mapping Legal Workflows Into Agency Handy

Agency Handy is built around the concept of a "service catalog" and an "order pipeline." The terminology is designed for agencies, not law firms. Here's the translation:

- **Services** become your practice areas or matter types (Family Law Consultation, Flat-Fee Will Package, Personal Injury Case Evaluation)
- **Leads** become prospective clients who have submitted intake information
- **Orders/Projects** become active matters
- **Tasks** become the action items within a matter (file complaint, order records, draft settlement demand)
- **Invoices** are just invoices

Once you reframe it that way, the setup is actually fast. I built out a mock solo practice in about 40 minutes.

**Step 1: Build your service catalog.** I set up three flat-fee services (initial consultation at $350, will and trust package at $1,500, demand letter at $500) and one hourly retainer entry. Each service gets a description, a price, and optional package add-ons. The service catalog is what clients see when they access your portal, so think of it as your "menu" for prospective and existing clients.

**Step 2: Create intake forms.** This is where Agency Handy earned points. The form builder is drag-and-drop. I built a new client intake form in about 15 minutes. Fields for name, contact info, matter type, opposing party, and a free-text description of the situation. When a prospect submits the form, they appear as a lead in your pipeline automatically. No manual data entry.

**Step 3: Set up your client portal.** Each client gets a login to their own portal view. They can see their matter status, uploaded documents, pending invoices, and outstanding tasks assigned to them (like signing documents or uploading records). The portal is white-labeled, which means your firm name appears, not "Agency Handy."

**Step 4: Connect your payment processor.** Agency Handy integrates with Stripe, PayPal, Wise, and manual bank transfer. I connected a Stripe account in about four minutes. Invoices generated within Agency Handy now include a payment link clients can click to pay directly.

## The Real Limitations

Agency Handy is not a legal practice management system. I need to say that clearly, because the gap matters.

It has no conflict check module. It has no court date calendar that syncs with bar association holidays. It does not have integrated legal research or document automation. There is no IOLTA trust accounting. These are features that tools like Clio or MyCase are built around. Agency Handy handles the front-end client relationship and business operations side. The legal practice machinery still lives elsewhere.

The terminology mismatch is a minor but real friction. When a client logs in and sees "Orders" where they expect "Matters," that can create confusion. The sidebar labels can be renamed, which helps, but you have to actually remember to do it and be consistent.

On Tier 1, there is no custom email domain. Your client communications come from Agency Handy's sending domain. That is fixable by upgrading to Tier 3, but it is a gap on the base tier.

One recurring complaint in the AppSumo reviews: SMTP customization for outbound emails went through a rebuild period and was temporarily unreliable. The vendor addressed this in their updates and as of the March 2026 campaign post it is marked as resolved, but worth knowing as recent history.

Time tracking exists in Agency Handy, but it is a basic timer and work log. It is not a billable hours tracker with automatic rate calculations per matter type. If you do hourly billing at different rates for different clients, you will likely still need your existing billing system for that math.
