## The Problem: Your Clients Have No Home Base

I spent several weeks testing SuiteDash against the kind of solo practice scenario I see most often: a general or family law attorney juggling intake, active matters, invoicing, and client communication across four or five separate tools. The combined monthly cost for a decent scheduling app, an e-signature service, a basic invoicing tool, and a client-facing messaging channel can easily run $60 to $90 per month. That adds up to over $1,000 per year, and none of those tools talk to each other.

SuiteDash packages all of that into one white-labeled platform your clients log into under your firm's branding. Here is what I focused on during testing:

1. **Client portal setup:** I configured a matter as a "project" and invited a test client. They saw a clean dashboard showing their matter phase, shared documents, and outstanding invoices. Zero "what's my case status?" emails needed.
2. **Retainer and e-signature flow:** I drafted a retainer agreement inside SuiteDash, sent it to the client through the portal, they signed it with e-signature, and I collected the initial payment on the same screen. That whole sequence ran in under 10 minutes.
3. **Appointment booking:** There is a built-in scheduling page that connects to Google Calendar. Basic conflict detection, direct booking link. It is not as polished as TidyCal, but it covers the core need without a separate subscription.

## The Verdict

The interface is busy. I am not going to pretend otherwise. Six years of feature development are all visible at once, and finding the appointment scheduling page for the first time took me about ten minutes of clicking. A non-technical attorney will need a Sunday afternoon to get this configured properly.

At $99 per month, I would tell a solo attorney to keep their existing tools. The monthly math does not pencil out unless they are billing at serious volume. At $159 one-time, the math is completely different. Two to three months of the tool subscriptions SuiteDash replaces and it has paid for itself.
